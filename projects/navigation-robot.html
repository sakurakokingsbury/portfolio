Autonomous Navigation Robot

Designed and built a fully autonomous robot capable of navigating an obstacle course using ultrasonic sensing and coordinate-based pathfinding, completing the final course in 1 minute 21 seconds with zero collisions.

Robotics Mechanical Design Embedded Systems SolidWorks Arduino

Project Overview

This project focused on designing and manufacturing an autonomous robot that could safely navigate a constrained arena, avoid obstacles, and stop at designated target locations. The system combined custom mechanical design, embedded electronics, and sensor-based navigation into a compact final build.

The robot used ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection and real-time coordinate data to move toward target points while staying within competition constraints.

Mechanical Design

The chassis was designed as a single printed part to reduce assembly complexity, minimize print failures, and keep the robot compact. The final geometry included integrated ultrasonic sensor mounts, an OLED display mount, a GPS float socket, and a crate-style compartment for the electronics.

This approach improved wire management, reduced bolt count, and kept the robot close to the footprint of the base chassis while still securely holding all required components.

Final chassis drawing and integrated mounting features.

Design Iteration

One of the most important parts of this project was the repeated design iteration. The chassis went through multiple revisions to improve stability, fit, manufacturability, and sensor performance.

Early Iterations

Focused on basic fit, board mounting, and creating an initial sensor mount for testing.

Mid Iterations

Added more ultrasonic sensors, adjusted board placement, and improved chassis layout for increased sensing coverage.

Later Iterations

Improved motor stability, reduced wire interference, strengthened sensor mounts, and adapted the design to replace the breadboard with a PCB.

Final Design

Introduced a compact crate-style structure, tighter sensor mounts, OLED display support, and a GPS float socket for reliable final performance.

Electronics and System Architecture

  • Wemos D1R1 (ESP8266) microcontroller
  • 3 ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection
  • Motor driver and DC motors for movement
  • OLED screen for real-time coordinate display
  • Custom PCB for power and ground distribution

The electrical layout was kept organized with a color-coded wiring scheme and zip ties to improve readability, maintenance, and reliability.

System wiring and board-to-sensor connections.

Autonomous Navigation Logic

The robot used coordinate-based target navigation combined with real-time obstacle avoidance. Its software continuously calculated movement relative to target positions while using ultrasonic sensor readings to detect walls and obstacles and adjust direction when needed.

This allowed the robot to move autonomously through the arena while remaining collision-free during the final run.

Challenges and Engineering Decisions

Sensor mounts were too loose

I revised the mount geometry and tightened tolerances so the ultrasonic sensors stayed secure during testing and final operation.

Wires interfered with moving parts

I helped improve wire routing and added structural protection to keep wires away from the wheels.

Motors shifted during operation

The chassis was widened and stabilized to reduce movement and improve repeatable driving.

Final design constraints changed

When breadboards were no longer allowed, the design was adapted to use a PCB while still maintaining a compact and organized layout.
